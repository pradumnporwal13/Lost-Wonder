Kanchi Kailasanathar Temple, Tamil Nadu

Situated in Kanchipuram, Tamil Nadu, the Kanchi Kailasanathar Temple is one of the oldest temples in the region. Built in the 8th century by the Pallava dynasty, the temple showcases the early Dravidian style of architecture and houses elaborately carved pillars and sculptures.

Chausath Yogini Temple, Madhya Pradesh

Situated in Jabalpur, Madhya Pradesh, the Chausath Yogini Temple is dedicated to the sixty-four Yogini goddesses. Constructed in the 10th century, this circular temple is one of the few surviving Yogini temples in India and features captivating sculptures of the Yoginis.This temple is so named because of the presence of multitude of shivalingas inside its cells. This circular temple is one among the very few such temples in India.

Osian Temples, Rajasthan

Located in the Thar Desert of Rajasthan, the Osian Temples are a group of beautifully carved Hindu and Jain temples. Dating back to the 8th to 11th centuries, these temples showcase exquisite architecture and intricate sculptures, highlighting the artistic prowess of the time.
